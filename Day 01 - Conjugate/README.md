## 4. Important: Verb Position (Ø¬Ø§ÛŒÚ¯Ø§Ù‡ ÙØ¹Ù„)

> **Teaching note:** This is a CRITICAL rule in German! In a simple sentence, the conjugated verb ALWAYS goes in position 2. This is different from English, so pay attention!

In a simple German sentence, the **conjugated verb is always in position 2**.

**What does "position 2" mean?**
- It means the second element in the sentence (not necessarily the second word)
- The first position can be the subject, an adverb, a time expression, etc.
- The verb MUST be in the second position

### Examples:

| Sentence | Position 1 | Position 2 (Verb) | Rest |
|----------|------------|-------------------|------|
| Ich gehe nach Hause. | Ich (I) | gehe (go) | nach Hause (home) |
| Heute arbeite ich. | Heute (Today) | arbeite (work) | ich (I) |
| Du kommst aus Iran. | Du (You) | kommst (come) | aus Iran (from Iran) |

**Key insight:** Notice in the second example, "Heute arbeite ich" - the subject `ich` moved to the end because `Heute` took position 1. The verb `arbeite` stayed in position 2!
