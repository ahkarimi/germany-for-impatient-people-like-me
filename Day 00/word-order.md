# German Word Order (A1 Level)

## The Golden Rule: Verb in Position 2

Okay, so here's the thing I need to remember: **In German main sentences, the verb is ALWAYS in Position 2.**

Not "the second word" — but the **second slot**. This is important because sometimes there might be multiple words in a slot.

---

## Basic Example: "I eat pizza"

Let me start simple. In English, I'd say: "I eat pizza."

The verb is "eat."

Now, let me translate this to German:

- **Subject** = `Ich`
- **Verb** = `esse`
- **Object** = `Pasta`

So I place the verb in slot 2:

```
Slot 1: Ich
Slot 2: esse
Slot 3: ... everything else
```

The sentence becomes: **`Ich esse Pizza.`**

---

## When Something Goes in Slot 1 First

Now let's try: **"Today I eat pizza."**

The German word for "today" is `heute`.

Here's what I need to remember:
- **Slot 1** can be anything → `heute`
- **Slot 2** must be the verb → `esse`
- **Slot 3** will be the rest

So the correct pattern is:

**`Heute esse ich Pizza.`**

*(Literally: "today eat I pizza" — yeah, sounds weird to me too, but that's German!)*

---

## Slot 1 Can Be ANYTHING!

This is super important: **Slot 1 can be anything!** It doesn't have to be the subject. Let me show myself with different examples using the same sentence idea.

### Example Sentence: "I eat pizza today at home."

**Ingredients:**
- `ich` (subject)
- `esse` (verb)
- `Pizza` (object)
- `heute` (time)
- `zu Hause` (place)

### Option 1: Subject in Slot 1 (most common)
```
Slot 1: ich (subject)
Slot 2: esse (verb)
Slot 3: Pizza heute zu Hause
```
**`Ich esse Pizza heute zu Hause.`**

### Option 2: Time in Slot 1
```
Slot 1: heute (time)
Slot 2: esse (verb)
Slot 3: ich Pizza zu Hause
```
**`Heute esse ich Pizza zu Hause.`**

### Option 3: Place in Slot 1
```
Slot 1: zu Hause (place)
Slot 2: esse (verb)
Slot 3: ich Pizza heute
```
**`Zu Hause esse ich Pizza heute.`**

### Option 4: Object in Slot 1 (less common, but possible!)
```
Slot 1: Pizza (object)
Slot 2: esse (verb)
Slot 3: ich heute zu Hause
```
**`Pizza esse ich heute zu Hause.`**

---

## More Examples with Different Slot 1 Options

### Sentence: "I learn German tomorrow at school."

**Option A - Subject first:**
**`Ich lerne morgen in der Schule Deutsch.`**

**Option B - Time first:**
**`Morgen lerne ich in der Schule Deutsch.`**

**Option C - Place first:**
**`In der Schule lerne ich morgen Deutsch.`**

### Sentence: "I cook pasta with my friend."

**Option A - Subject first:**
**`Ich koche mit meinem Freund Pasta.`**

**Option B - Manner first:**
**`Mit meinem Freund koche ich Pasta.`**

**Option C - Object first:**
**`Pasta koche ich mit meinem Freund.`**

---

## Key Takeaway

**Remember:** Whatever goes in Slot 1, the verb STILL goes in Slot 2, and everything else follows in Slot 3 (following TMP order when applicable).

The subject doesn't disappear — it just moves to Slot 3 if something else takes Slot 1!

---

## Time – Manner – Place (TMP)

This is how I order extra information in a sentence. The rule is:

**Time → Manner → Place**

### Examples:

- **Time**: `heute` (today), `morgen` (tomorrow), `am Morgen` (in the morning)
- **Manner**: `langsam` (slowly), `schnell` (quickly), `mit meinem Freund` (with my friend)
- **Place**: `zu Hause` (at home), `in der Schule` (at school), `in Berlin`

---

## Practice Example 1: "I learn German today at home."

Let me build this sentence step by step.

**Ingredients:**
- `ich` (subject)
- `lerne` (verb)
- `Deutsch` (object)
- `heute` (time)
- `zu Hause` (place)

**Step 1:** Verb goes in slot 2 → `lerne`

**Step 2:** Subject goes in slot 1 → `ich`

**Step 3:** Apply TMP rule → Time (`heute`) → Place (`zu Hause`) → Object (`Deutsch`)

**Result:** **`Ich lerne heute zu Hause Deutsch.`**

---

## Practice Example 2: "I cook pasta tomorrow with my friend at home."

This one's a bit harder. Let me break it down:

**Ingredients:**
- `ich` (subject)
- `koche` (verb)
- `Pasta` (object)
- `morgen` (time)
- `mit meinem Freund` (manner)
- `zu Hause` (place)

**Step 1:** Identify the verb → `koche` → goes in slot 2

**Step 2:** Choose what goes in Slot 1

For simple sentences, Slot 1 can be anything — subject, time word, place word, etc. Let me keep it simple and start with the subject `ich`.

**Step 3:** Apply TMP rule:
- Time → `morgen`
- Manner → `mit meinem Freund`
- Place → `zu Hause`
- Object → `Pasta`

**Result:** **`Ich koche morgen mit meinem Freund zu Hause Pasta.`**

---

## Quick Recap

⭐ **Main sentence rule:** Verb = slot 2  
⭐ **Extra info order:** Time → Manner → Place
